## Backstory

Tink was born among the wandering kender of **Kendermore**, where stories, roads, and forgotten things are treated as part of the same great treasure. Like many kender, they grew up with little sense of ownership and less interest in staying put. What set Tink apart was an unusual fixation on lost relics, old battlefields, and half-forgotten histories. They were not driven by greed so much as fascination: every object seemed to carry a story, and every story seemed worth preserving.

At some point, as all kender eventually do, Tink simply… left. There was no grand farewell or defining moment—just a growing restlessness that turned into movement. What began as a short wandering became a long one. Driven by curiosity rather than direction, Tink drifted across roads, towns, and regions, eventually reaching **Solamnia** through little more than chance and persistence.

They survived the journey in typical kender fashion: “borrowing” what they needed along the way. Tink keeps a running list—loosely maintained—of all the people they owe money or items to, fully intending to repay them someday, though with no urgency or real plan for doing so. Along their travels, they also developed a reputation—unbeknownst to themselves—for slipping into private collections, storehouses, and even small museums to examine and “temporarily acquire” interesting artifacts for closer study.

Over time, this pattern of wandering theft-adjacent curiosity evolved into something unexpected. Tink’s habit of rifling through archives, overhearing conversations in inns and war camps, and reading whatever scraps of writing they could find made them, paradoxically, one of the more broadly informed minds in the region. They possess an unusual, almost instinctive grasp of history, political entanglements, and old Solamnic lore—not from study, but from accumulation. This is paired with an intuitive understanding of mechanisms and devices; locks, traps, siege contraptions, and strange relic-machines often make immediate sense to them once they’ve had their hands on them, despite their complete lack of formal education or discipline.

As a child and traveler, Tink wandered often, following rumors and roadside legends whenever they could. They learned early to move lightly, talk fast, and notice what others overlooked. They became especially interested in old knightly tales, battlefield relics, and artifacts said to have shaped the great conflicts of Krynn. Over time, that curiosity turned into a private habit of sketching and recording what they found, making Tink less of a thief in their own mind and more of a chronicler of wonders.

That habit eventually brought them to **Vogler** at the same time as the gathering for **Ser Ispin Greenshield’s funeral**. Tink had heard of Ispin’s legendary shield and, in true kender fashion, could not resist the chance to see it up close. They intended to examine it, sketch it, and perhaps “borrow” it long enough to study the details properly. They did not understand this as theft at all, nor did they grasp that it was in poor taste so soon after his death. To Tink, it was simply the natural impulse to admire an extraordinary thing by touching it, carrying it, and learning everything possible about it.

They were caught before they could make off with the shield. Once confronted by the party, Tink immediately relented, bewildered more than ashamed, and explained—quite sincerely—that they only wanted to look, measure, and record it. What could have become a serious theft instead became the beginning of an unlikely bond. The characters were too interesting to leave behind, and Tink, fascinated by their history and purpose, decided to stay with them out of pure curiosity.

From then on, Tink became less a burglar than an accidental companion: a chronicler, scout, and collector of stories. They want to record the deeds of the party not because they have been asked, but because they instinctively feel that the best stories in the world are the ones still unfolding.

Unbeknownst to Tink, their homeland of Kendermore now struggles under the growing threat of the Dragonarmies. Cut off by distance and their own wandering nature, they remain completely unaware of what is happening there. When they eventually learn the truth, it will reshape their purpose. What is now curiosity may become something sharper: a desire to find a relic, power, or legend great enough to return home not as a wanderer, but as someone capable of helping liberate it.

## Personality

Fizwick is bright, nosy, cheerful, and impossible to fully discourage. They ask questions constantly, often before it would be socially appropriate, and they move through danger with an almost alarming lack of fear. Their curiosity is not a pose; it is the engine of their entire life.

They are also oddly attentive. Beneath the chatter and impulsive wandering is a mind that notices details other people miss: who is lying, what changed in a room, which path has been recently used, which object “feels important.” That makes them more useful than they first appear, even when they are being infuriating.

### Personality Traits

- Curious to the point of compulsive.
- Cheerful even in tense situations.
- Talks too much when excited.
- Treats strange objects and places as invitations rather than warnings.
- Remembers stories, faces, and details better than people expect.

### Ideals

- **Wonder:** the world should be seen, recorded, and remembered.
- **Curiosity:** nothing important should go unexplored.
- **Story:** deeds matter because someone must tell them.
- **Freedom:** no road, door, or legend should remain closed forever.

### Bonds

- The stories Fizwick has collected and the ones still waiting to be told.
- The party, whom they have decided are worth following.
- Ispin Greenshield’s shield, which represents both legend and history.
- The northern wastes, which they long to explore but promised not to enter alone.

### Flaws

- Cannot resist touching, taking, or investigating things that fascinate them.
- Underestimates how offensive their “borrowing” can be to others.
- Prone to wandering off at the worst possible time.
- Makes promises casually, then treats them as sacred if someone else is truly worried.
- Curiosity can override caution.

## Hooks

- Fizwick joins the party because they are fascinated by their history and want to chronicle their deeds.
- They first become involved by attempting to study, sketch, and “borrow” Ispin’s shield at the funeral in Vogler.
- Their kender nature makes them useful as a scout and source of unexpected information, even when they are a liability.
- Their habit of recording events gives the party an in-world chronicler for their journey.
- They want to explore the northern wastes someday, but promised someone they would never go there alone, so the later campaign journey gives them a safe chance to do it.
- Their curiosity will repeatedly pull them toward relics, ruins, and secrets tied to Krynn’s larger history.
