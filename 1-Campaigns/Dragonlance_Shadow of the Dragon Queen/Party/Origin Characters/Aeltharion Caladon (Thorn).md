## Backstory

### Early life

Eltharion Vanyarin was born to Lorac Caladon, Speaker of the Stars, and was the younger brother of Alhana Starbreeze. In the halls of Silvanost, he was known first for gentleness rather than war: a gifted singer, poet, and dancer whose grace seemed made for the autumn light of his people. He was deeply loyal to his family and was present in Silvanost during the final days before the fall, when Lorac took the Dragon Orb in desperation and attempted to turn its power against the invading Dragonarmies. 

He initially aspired to follow in his father's footsteps and become a sorcerer of the High Tower, studying at the Tower of High Sorcery to master the arcane arts as Lorac once had. Among those closest to him was the Sylvanesti elder Zhelsuel, a trusted advisor of the court who shared a warm, almost familial bond with Eltharion, acting as a guiding presence and something of an uncle during his youth.

### War comes to Sylvanost

Eventually Sylvanost was beseiged by the Dragon Army. Unlike many of the Silvanesti who fled with Alhana to Southern Ergoth, Eltharion refused to leave Silvanost. He chose instead to remain behind in the dying forest, unwilling to abandon what remained of his father's realm even as the Dragonarmies closed in and the land began to twist under the growing influence of Lorac's Nightmare.

Before the final collapse, Eltharion shared a last farewell with Alhana at the edge of the royal grounds. She urged him to come with her and the refugees, but he would not be swayed. Their parting was strained with fear and grief—Alhana torn between duty to their people and concern for her brother, and Eltharion resolute yet visibly shaken. In the end, she left him behind, and he watched her depart with the surviving Silvanesti, knowing it might be the last time he saw his sister alive.

Sometime during the war, after the first waves of refugees had already fled, Zhelsuel carried out what he saw as his final duty to their people. Alongside other scholars and remnants of House Mystic, he was sent on an expedition to search for ancient ruins and lost Silvanesti magic in distant lands. Though presented as a mission of recovery and hope, Zhelsuel did not hide the truth from Eltharion. In a quiet, private moment before his departure, he confided that this was exile in all but name—a way to remove those who could no longer defend their homeland while preserving the illusion of purpose. With that, he took his leave, offering what guidance and farewell he could, knowing they might never see one another again.

Eltharion witnessed the catastrophe that followed. The Orb did not save Silvanesti; instead, it shattered the boundary between nightmare and waking, and Lorac was consumed by the corruption that became Lorac's Nightmare. In the chaos of that magical catastrophe, Eltharion saw firsthand how sorcerous power, unchecked and prideful, could destroy an entire kingdom. From that night onward, he came to loathe sorcery and all who trafficked in such powers, believing it to be the stain that ruined his father and damned his homeland.

### Hell on Ansalon

When the Dragonarmies struck after the disaster, Eltharion was caught in the collapse and violence that followed the orb's eruption. The explosion and the panic of the fall left him stranded and vulnerable, and he was taken captive by the Dragonarmies amid the ruin of Silvanesti. What followed was a long captivity of humiliation and cruelty, where he was treated as prey rather than a son of Silvanesti's royal house. His captors mutilated him, leaving his ears scarred, one eye destroyed by draconic acid, and one leg broken so badly it never fully healed. He escaped or was eventually freed only after enduring months of torment, but the boy who once sang beneath the stars did not return unchanged.

He was dragged from camp to camp by both the Red Dragon Army and the Blue Dragon Army, eventually carried to Taman Busuk, and later to the Red Dragon Army encampment in the woods west of Vogler. At last, Eltharion found a way to escape. Wounded, malnourished, and half-dead from the road, he fled toward Vogler. Along the way, draconians took up the chase, but he killed them with his bare hands before they could bring him down. When he finally emerged from the treeline, it was moments before the betrayal at High Hill, and he came limping out of the woods as battle was about to begin.

Even now, Eltharion carries the bitterness of that night: against dragonkind, he shows no mercy, and against sorcery he shows no trust. The memory of Lorac's failure, the ruin of Silvanesti, and the price paid by his family left him with a hard, unforgiving edge. Yet beneath that armor remains the old Silvanesti soul—still capable of song, poetry, and quiet grief for the kingdom that was lost. He has since taken the name **Thorn**, rejecting his royal identity because he no longer sees himself as a prince of Silvanesti, but as something sharpened and broken by its ruin—something that survives only by wounding what comes too close.

## Personality

Eltharion Vanyarin presents as controlled, distant, and sharply observant. Years of captivity and the destruction of Silvanesti have stripped away the open gentleness he once displayed, leaving a guarded exterior that prioritizes survival, assessment, and preemptive threat elimination. He rarely speaks more than necessary in unfamiliar company, and when he does, his tone is measured and precise.

Despite this, remnants of his former life remain visible in subtle ways: an instinctive sensitivity to music, brief moments of stillness when he observes beauty in nature, and an unguarded calm when he believes he is alone or among trusted companions. These fragments rarely last long before vigilance reasserts itself.

In rejection of the arcane catastrophe that destroyed Silvanesti and consumed his father, Eltharion has turned away from structured sorcery and its traditions. Instead, he has returned to a more primal understanding of elven life—one rooted in instinct, nature, and the living world rather than formalized magical study. This shift has shaped his identity as a ranger, drawing on the natural rhythms of forest, beast, and terrain as a form of discipline and power. His abilities are not learned from towers or tomes, but from survival, observation, and an inherited attunement to the natural world that he associates with what his people were before their fall into arcane excess.

Hatred dominates much of Eltharion's waking thought. The destruction of Silvanesti, his captivity, and the mutilation he endured have left him with a persistent, consuming rage that rarely fully subsides. This is especially pronounced when dealing with dragons, draconians, and any servants of the Dragonarmies; in their presence, his judgment becomes immediate and often merciless, with restraint becoming difficult to maintain. The same instability arises around lunar sorcerers and practitioners tied to the Towers of High Sorcery, whom he associates with the arcane failures that led to his father's downfall. In such moments, his rationality can erode into fixation, making him prone to reckless or disproportionately violent responses.

He is highly disciplined in movement and perception, shaped by both his elven heritage and ranger training. He constantly scans environments for exits, threats, and ambush points. His trust is difficult to earn, but once given, it is absolute and difficult to break.

### Traits

- Speaks sparingly, preferring observation over participation.
- Unnervingly calm in danger due to prolonged trauma conditioning.
- Displays heightened awareness of surroundings at all times.
- Shows unexpected sensitivity to music, rhythm, and natural sound.
- Struggles to reconcile compassion with learned survival brutality.

### Ideals

- **Retribution:** Those who destroyed Silvanesti and its people must be answered for.
- **Survival:** Weakness is exploited; endurance is proof of worth.
- **Preservation:** Innocents must not suffer what he endured.
- **Truth:** Illusion and deception (especially magical) are inherently dangerous and corrupting.

### Bonds

- Alhana Starbreeze: his sister, last known anchor to his family and identity.
- Lorac Caladon: his father, both revered and condemned for the catastrophe of the Orb.
- Silvanesti homeland: a lost kingdom he is compelled to return to or understand.
- The memory of those left behind in Silvanesti's fall.

### Flaws

- Extreme hostility toward draconians and dragonkin; prone to lethal overreaction.
- Deep distrust of arcane magic and those who use it.
- Survivor's guilt manifests as self-imposed isolation and emotional detachment.
- Refusal to fully reintegrate with elven society due to shame and perceived failure.
- Difficulty distinguishing mercy from weakness in combat decisions.

## Story Hooks

### Vengeance Against Dragonarmies

Eltharion's captivity and mutilation by the Dragonarmies defines much of his present identity. The opportunity to operate alongside the Knights of Solamnia in Kalaman offers direct and repeated contact with draconians and dragon forces. This is not only strategic alignment but personal retribution. Any campaign against dragonkin becomes both mission and compulsion, making him an exceptionally driven hunter but also a potential liability if restraint is required. 

In addition to his personal history, he is one of the few members of the party with firsthand exposure to the Dragonarmies' operations beyond the front lines, having been moved through multiple camps and regions during his captivity. This gives him rare, practical insight into their structure, movements, and brutality across occupied territories, as well as knowledge of their methods gained from direct observation rather than secondhand reports.

### Scout and Tracker Role

Eltharion's elven heritage and survival conditioning make him an exceptional scout. He possesses heightened perception, natural tracking instincts, and environmental awareness well-suited to reconnaissance in hostile territory. He functions best ahead of the main force, identifying threats, routes, and ambushes. His value to armies of good lies in intelligence gathering and silent navigation rather than frontline leadership.

### Silvanesti Exile and Shame

He is aware that the Silvanesti survived as refugees, yet avoids seeking them out. His survival while Silvanesti suffered and his perceived failure to protect or remain with his people weigh heavily on him. This produces a strong avoidance response toward elven society and any situation where his identity as a Caladon prince might be revealed.

### Refugees of the Northern Wastes

Later in the story, the party encounters Silvanesti refugees in the northern wastes. Among them are individuals who may have known the royal family. Eltharion is not immediately recognized due to his mutilations and altered appearance. This creates a critical choice point: he may reveal his identity and reclaim his place, or remain anonymous and continue as a shadow of his former self. Recognition or concealment carries significant political and emotional consequences, both for him and the surviving Silvanesti.

### Internal Conflict Arc

Eltharion's journey is defined by the tension between vengeance and restoration. His path constantly tests whether he remains a weapon shaped by trauma or can recover the identity of Eltharion Vanyarin of Silvanost—son of Lorac, brother of Alhana, and heir to a broken legacy.
