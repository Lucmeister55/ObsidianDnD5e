## Worm

**Race:** Dragonborn (Black)
**Background:** Farmer
**Class:** Barbarian (Zealot)

## Backstory

Worm's story begins in strangeness. As a child, he was cast into Krynn during the great collision of worlds, a fate he neither understood nor controlled. From the moment of his arrival, whispers clung to him. The smallfolk, still haunted by dragon myths and the silence of the gods, called him a bad omen. Scholars and sages studied him with suspicion, unsettled by the subtle chromatic cast to his skin and eyes—traits he himself did not understand. To some, he was a harbinger of doom. To others, a messiah sent to break the godless silence.

It was that last belief which drew the attention of a faction of radical devotees of Paladine. In their fanatic search for signs, they seized upon Worm as a supposed vessel of prophecy. For a time, he was paraded as an omen of the god's return, treated as both idol and prisoner. But as their zeal grew more twisted, their faith in him soured. They branded him unworthy, a fraud, a living blasphemy. When their obsession curdled into hatred, they cast him off. Isolated, despised, and stripped of any protection, Worm was left vulnerable.

Rumors of the "god-touched boy” soon reached darker ears. He was captured and sold through the criminal underworld, paraded on a slave block where his strange features and cursed reputation marked him as both curiosity and liability. His buyers were the infamous Bakaris Uth Estide the Elder and his son, Bakaris the Younger.

To Worm, they were two sides of the same cruelty. The Elder, calculating and pitiless, saw him as little more than property—a tool to be worked until broken. The Younger was worse in many ways: arrogant, vain, and eager to prove himself through violence, he delighted in humiliating Worm, taking out his temper and frustrations on the boy whenever he could. Among the stables and muck, Worm was reduced to less than human, a slave in all but name, stripped of dignity and treated as a plaything for their malice.

## Hooks

- Worm is a vessel between mortal and divine, light and darkness.  
- Paladine: radiant visions demanding resilience, justice, and selflessness.  
- Takhisis: shadowed comfort, offering power, control, and revenge.  
- Worm's choices could affect the balance between Light and Darkness in Krynn.
