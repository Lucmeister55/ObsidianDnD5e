# Backgrounds
When you make a character for a Dragonlance campaign, choose one of the backgrounds in this section or select a [background](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/phb-2014/personality-and-background#Backgrounds#Backgrounds) from the Player’s Handbook or another source.

This section presents two new backgrounds for characters from the world of Krynn: the [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Backgrounds#Knight of Solamnia|Knight of Solamnia]] and the [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Backgrounds#Mage of High Sorcery|Mage of High Sorcery]].

### Bonus Feats

Whatever background you choose for a character in a Dragonlance campaign, you gain bonus feats, as detailed below. Characters involved in the War of the Lance or other conflicts on Krynn are changed by the dramatic experiences they face.

#### 1st-Level Bonus Feat

If you select the [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Backgrounds#Knight of Solamnia|Knight of Solamnia]] or the [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Backgrounds#Mage of High Sorcery|Mage of High Sorcery]] background, you gain the feat specified in that background. If the background you choose doesn’t provide a feat, you gain a bonus feat of your choice from the following list (a parenthesis tells you where to find the feat):

*   [Skilled](https://www.dndbeyond.com/feats/skilled) (Player’s Handbook)
*   [Tough](https://www.dndbeyond.com/feats/tough) (Player’s Handbook)

#### 4th-Level Bonus Feat

At 4th level, you gain another bonus feat of your choice from either the 1st-level list above or the following list (a parenthesis tells you where to find the feat):

*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Adept of the Black Robes|Adept of the Black Robes]] (this chapter)
*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Adept of the Red Robes|Adept of the Red Robes]] (this chapter)
*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Adept of the White Robes|Adept of the White Robes]] (this chapter)
*   [Alert](https://www.dndbeyond.com/feats/alert) (Player’s Handbook)
*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Divinely Favored|Divinely Favored]] (this chapter)
*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Knight of the Crown|Knight of the Crown]] (this chapter)
*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Knight of the Rose|Knight of the Rose]] (this chapter)
*   [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Knight of the Sword|Knight of the Sword]] (this chapter)
*   [Mobile](https://www.dndbeyond.com/feats/mobile) (Player’s Handbook)
*   [Sentinel](https://www.dndbeyond.com/feats/sentinel) (Player’s Handbook)
*   [War Caster](https://www.dndbeyond.com/feats/war-caster) (Player’s Handbook)

You gain this bonus feat at 4th level regardless of your background—even if you have the Knight of Solamnia or Mage of High Sorcery background. You must meet all prerequisites if the feat you choose has any.

### Knight of Solamnia

_Prerequisite: Dragonlance Campaign_

You have trained to be a valorous warrior known as a Knight of Solamnia. Strict rules guide your every action, and you work to uphold them as you strive to defend the weak and oppose evil. Your honor is as important to you as your life.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics, Survival

**Languages:** Two of your choice

**Equipment:** An insignia of rank, a [deck of cards](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/deck-ofcards), a [set of common clothes](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/set-ofcommonclothes), and a [pouch](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/pouch) containing 10 gp

#### Feature: Squire of Solamnia

You gain the [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Squire of Solamnia|Squire of Solamnia]] feat (presented later in this chapter).

In addition, the Knights of Solamnia provide you free, modest lodging and food at any of their fortresses or encampments.

#### Building a Knight of Solamnia Character

Any class that has martial prowess can be a good fit in the Knights of Solamnia. Fighters and paladins make up the bulk of the knighthood’s forces. Clerics (often with the [War domain](https://www.dndbeyond.com/subclasses/war-domain)) can also be found among the knights’ ranks.

For a more unusual take on a Knight of Solamnia character, consider playing a bard of the [College of Valor](https://www.dndbeyond.com/subclasses/college-of-valor) (or the [College of Swords](https://www.dndbeyond.com/subclasses/college-of-swords) from Xanathar’s Guide to Everything) or a barbarian devoted to the ideals of the nature god Habbakuk (perhaps adopting the [Path of the Zealot](https://www.dndbeyond.com/subclasses/path-of-the-zealot) from Xanathar’s Guide to Everything).

_**Knight of Solamnia Trinkets.**_ When you make your character, roll once on the Knight of Solamnia Trinkets table instead of on the [Trinkets table](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/phb-2014/equipment#Trinkets#Trinkets) in the Player’s Handbook for your starting trinket.

### Mage of High Sorcery

_Prerequisite: Dragonlance Campaign_

Your talent for magic came to the attention of the Mages of High Sorcery, an organization of spellcasters that studies magic and prevents its misuse. You’ve trained among the Mages, but whether you’ll face the dangerous test required to become a full member of the group remains to be determined. Your passion for studying magic has likely already predisposed you toward one of the organization’s three orders: the benevolent Order of the White Robes, the balance-pursuing Order of the Red Robes, or the ruthless Order of the Black Robes.

In the world of Krynn, many refer to the Mages of High Sorcery as the Wizards of High Sorcery. The organization accepts more than wizards, though, with sorcerers, warlocks, and other spellcasters included among their ranks.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Arcana, History

**Languages:** Two of your choice

**Equipment:** A [bottle of colored ink](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/bottle-ofcoloredink), an [ink pen](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/ink-pen), a [set of common clothes](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/set-ofcommonclothes), and a [pouch](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/pouch) containing 10 gp

#### Feature: Initiate of High Sorcery

You gain the [[1. Campaigns/Dragonlance_Shadow of the Dragon Queen/DBB Import/Dragonlance_ Shadow of the Dragon Queen/Chapter 1_ Character Creation/Feats#Initiate of High Sorcery|Initiate of High Sorcery]] feat (presented later in this chapter).

In addition, the Mages of High Sorcery provide you with free, modest lodging and food indefinitely at any occupied Tower of High Sorcery and for one night at the home of an organization member.

#### Building a Mage of High Sorcery Character

Mages of High Sorcery are typically sorcerers, warlocks, or wizards and might have any subclass. Spellcasters who gain their magic through devotion are less likely to be welcomed among the traditionalist mages. Nevertheless, the Mages of High Sorcery are shrewd, and they rarely let unique opportunities or individuals pass them by. Even members of martial classes who train in magic might find a rare place among the group’s three orders.

_**Mage of High Sorcery Trinkets.**_ When you make your character, roll once on the Mage of High Sorcery Trinkets table instead of on the [Trinkets table](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/phb-2014/equipment#Trinkets#Trinkets) in the Player’s Handbook for your starting trinket.

| 5 | A pouch or spellbook emblazoned with the triple moon symbol of the Mages of High Sorcery |
