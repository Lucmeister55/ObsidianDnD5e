*Withstand the Horrors of Frigid Wastelands*

Winter Walkers hone their craft in the bleak and frozen wilds of places like Icewind Dale. These ruthless, rimed Rangers hunt monsters that haunt arctic wastelands, eventually becoming frigid terrors themselves. Winter Walkers are well versed in the phenomena of Icewind Dale, including the latent magic of fallen Netherese cities, endemic monsters like [yetis](/3-Mechanics/CLI/bestiary/monstrosity/yeti-xmm.md) and crag cats, and the rising threat of Underdark invaders. Due to their cold pragmatism, terrifying magic, and mastery of the region, Winter Walkers are regarded with equal parts respect and fear. Ten-Towns citizens say that Winter Walkers' frequent exposure to malignant entities gives them their fearsome powers. Many Reghed nomads, on the other hand, believe that nature spirits bestow on Winter Walkers a unique curse.

## Subclass Features

### Frigid Explorer (Level 3)

You gain the following benefits.

**Biting Cold.** Damage from your weapon attacks, Ranger spells, and Ranger features ignores [Resistance](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/resistance-xphb.md) to Cold damage.

**Frost Resistance.** You have [Resistance](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/resistance-xphb.md) to Cold damage.

**Polar Strikes.** When you hit a creature with an attack roll using a weapon, you can deal an extra `dice:1d4|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d4`) Cold damage to the target, which can take this extra damage only once per turn. When you reach Ranger level 11, this extra damage increases to `dice:1d6|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d6`).

### Hunter's Rime (Level 3)

Ice rimes you and your prey, protecting you and slowing them. When you cast [Hunter's Mark](/3-Mechanics/CLI/spells/hunters-mark-xphb.md), you gain [Temporary Hit Points](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/temporary-hit-points-xphb.md) equal to `dice:1d10|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d10`) plus your [Ranger](/3-Mechanics/CLI/classes/ranger-xphb.md) level.

Additionally, while a creature is marked by your [Hunter's Mark](/3-Mechanics/CLI/spells/hunters-mark-xphb.md), it can't take the [Disengage](/3-Mechanics/CLI/actions.md#Disengage) action.

### Fortifying Soul (Level 7)

Your experience surviving harrowing environments allows you to bolster your allies in addition to yourself. As a [Magic](/3-Mechanics/CLI/actions.md#Magic) action, choose a number of creatures you can see equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of one). Each chosen creature regains [Hit Points](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/hit-points-xphb.md) equal to `dice:1d10|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d10`) plus your Ranger level and has [Advantage](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/advantage-xphb.md) on saving throws to avoid or end the [Frightened](/3-Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Frightened) condition for 1 hour.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a [Long Rest](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md).

### Chilling Retribution (Level 11)

When a creature hits you with an attack roll, you can take a [Reaction](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/reaction-xphb.md) to force the creature to make a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, the target has the [Stunned](/3-Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Stunned) condition until the end of your next turn. While the target is [Stunned](/3-Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Stunned), its [Speed](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/speed-xphb.md) is reduced to 0 feet.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a [Long Rest](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md).

### Frozen Haunt (Level 15)

When you cast [Hunter's Mark](/3-Mechanics/CLI/spells/hunters-mark-xphb.md), you can adopt a ghostly, snowy form. This form lasts until the spell ends, and while you are in this form, you gain the following benefits. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a [Long Rest](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/long-rest-xphb.md) unless you expend a level 4+ spell slot (no action required).

**Frozen Soul.** You have [Immunity](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/immunity-xphb.md) to Cold damage. When you first adopt this form and at the start of each of your subsequent turns, each creature of your choice in a 15-foot [Emanation](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/emanation-area-of-effect-xphb.md) originating from you takes `dice:2d4|noform|noparens|avg` (`2d4`) Cold damage.

**Partially Incorporeal.** You have [Immunity](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/immunity-xphb.md) to the [Grappled](/3-Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Grappled), [Prone](/3-Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Prone), and [Restrained](/3-Mechanics/CLI/conditions.md#Restrained) conditions. You can move through creatures and objects as if they were [Difficult Terrain](/3-Mechanics/CLI/variant-rules/difficult-terrain-xphb.md), but you take `dice:1d10|noform|noparens|avg` (`1d10`) Force damage if you end your turn inside a creature or an object. If the form ends while you are inside a creature or an object, you are shunted to the nearest unoccupied space.
