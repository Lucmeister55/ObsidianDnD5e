## Ser Alric

**Race:** Human  
**Background:** Knight of Solamnia  
**Class:** Fighter (Cavalier)
**Starting player**: Thomas
![[sturm-brightblade-sturm-brightblade-25158415-422-504.jpg]]
### Backstory

Alric was born in a quiet, unremarkable village. As a young teen, he lived a peaceful but ordinary life—until a violent raid struck his home. He witnessed devastation and loss on a scale he had never imagined. Just when all seemed lost, a company of Solamnic Knights, led by Ser Ispin Greenshield, arrived and drove off the attackers. Alric survived thanks to their intervention.

The experience left a deep mark on him. He saw the courage, discipline, and selflessness of the knights firsthand and vowed that, if given the chance, he would dedicate his life to their ideals. After the raid, he sought out Ser Ispin, who reluctantly agreed to take him on as a squire. Under Ispin's tutelage, Alric trained tirelessly, proving himself in combat, strategy, and leadership.

Shortly before the campaign begins, Ser Ispin Greenshield led a small mission to escort a noble's convoy through a relatively quiet region. Alric, eager to prove himself, begged to join the escort, but Ser Ispin shook his head.

> "This is a routine task. You stay here. Your place is with me when the real test comes.”

Reluctantly, Alric obeyed, remaining behind while the knights departed. Days later, the convoy was found destroyed in a mysterious manner: wagons smashed, horses gone, and bodies marked by strange, nightmarish purple burns that seemed alive. Ser Ispin had died shielding a young page. The incident haunted Alric, leaving him determined to live up to the ideals of knighthood.

Alric's path to knighthood is fueled by loyalty, courage, and the desire to protect others, even when forces beyond understanding intervene.
### Hooks

- Alric's journey through the ranks of the Solamnic knights offers a path of responsibility, challenge, and personal growth.  
- Each promotion, mission, and assignment tests courage, judgment, and loyalty.  
- Progression shaped by the Order he serves, which manifest as changeable feats:  

| Order | Focus / Flavor |
|-------|----------------|
| Order of the Crown | Traditional, honor-bound knights; loyalty, justice, large-scale command. |
| Order of the Sword | Combat specialists; valor-focused, frontline-oriented. |
| Order of the Rose | Courtly and diplomatic knights; focus on noble protection, ceremonies, and investigation. |

### Suggested Level Progression

| Level | Position / Rank | Description / Hook |
|-------|----------------|------------------|
| 4 | Knight of the Crown | Formally knighted. Focus on leadership, honor, and responsibility. Minor independent duties. |
| 5 | Lieutenant | Lead small troop, junior commander, participate in Orders' councils. |
| 6 | Knight Banneret | Commands company-sized force. Battlefield leadership, tactical planning, mentoring squires. |
| 7 | Captain | Oversees multiple units or garrisons. Strategic responsibilities, combat and diplomacy. |
| 8 | Knight of the Realm | High-ranking knight. Independent campaigns, lead elite forces, regional politics impact. |

| 10 | Marshal | Key advisor to Orders and governing body. Lead elite operations, shape strategy. |

## Fizwick

**Race:** Kender
**Background:** Criminal
**Class:** Rogue (Thief)
**Starting player**: Victor
![[Kender Skirmisher.webp]]
### Backstory

Fizwick drifts through the world like a leaf on the wind. They don't know where they came from—nor do they seem to care. Ask about their family, their hometown, or even their own childhood, and Fiz will grin, shrug, and say, "Does it matter? I'm here now, aren't I?” Their memories of the past are hazy at best, disappearing as quickly as they surface.

What Fiz does have is an uncanny intuition. They don't study maps or train endlessly, and they don't need formal instruction—the right path, the correct maneuver, or the clever solution always seems to come naturally. Whether picking a lock, avoiding danger, or navigating a crowded city, Fiz just "knows” what to do, as if some invisible hand whispers guidance. Their intuition is inseparable from their incredible luck: improbable events bend in their favor, hazards resolve themselves, and opportunities present themselves at exactly the right moment. Fate itself seems to favor them.

Beneath the playful, whimsical exterior lies a remarkable adaptability. A knight's skill, a rogue's cunning, a mage's spark—Fiz can rise to whatever the moment demands. They are, in truth, an avatar of Reorx, god of creation, a living instrument of ingenuity and possibility. Through them, divine intuition flows, guiding them to solutions that feel effortless, as if the world itself conspires to make their creativity succeed.

Fiz's actions, though whimsical or accidental, often ripple far beyond their understanding. A trinket pocketed, a doorway explored, a seemingly random gesture—they can alter the balance of power, save lives, or set history on a new course. Small, carefree, and seemingly inconsequential, Fiz embodies the archetype of the "hobbit who changes the world,” a reminder that intuition, luck, and curiosity can shape creation itself.

Though they rarely plan ahead, Fiz is drawn to those who fight for justice, order, and protection. Knights, adventurers, and heroes provide structure to their chaos, giving their luck and intuition purpose. By walking alongside them, Fiz applies their innate sense, curiosity, and Reorx's blessing to protect the world, uncover lost relics, and tip the scales of fate—all while laughing, marveling, and sometimes inventing entirely new solutions on a whim.
### Hooks

- Curiosity and playful nature draw Fiz into forbidden or intriguing places.  
- Divine guidance allows effortless improvisation and impactful decisions.  
- Neutral alignment enables navigation of complex moral landscapes.  
- Can adapt to fill gaps wherever the party requires—scouting, crafting, fighting, casting spells.  
- Small, whimsical actions can have monumental consequences.

## Elrohir

**Race:** Wood Elf
**Background:** Guide
**Class:** Ranger (Hunter)
**Starting player**: Smoothie

![[D5aNHkbWsAA1V7T.jpg]]
### Backstory

Elrohir Vanyarin was once known among his kin as a gentle soul. In the verdant groves of Silvanesti, his voice often carried on the wind in song, and his nimble steps graced festivals with dance. He had a gift for poetry, weaving words as delicately as the strings of his lute, and he delighted in sharing music under the starlit canopy of his homeland. Friends and family alike knew him for his warmth and artistry—an elf who embraced life's beauty in all its forms.

That life was stolen when the Dragon Armies descended upon Silvanesti. Elrohir was captured in the chaos, dragged from his home, and thrown into a nightmare of cruelty. His captors found amusement in torment, subjecting him to isolation, deprivation, and sadistic games where survival meant fighting through traps or fleeing from beasts. For months he was treated not as a person, but as prey.

The scars of that captivity are more than emotional. His ears—once proud and elegant like all of his kin—were mangled by tormentors, a cruel attempt to strip him of his elven grace. His body never fully healed from the injuries he endured; he still carries a subtle limp from a shattered leg bone that mended poorly. Though his natural elven poise offsets the worst of it, the flaw gnaws at his pride, a constant reminder of the dignity that was stolen from him. Lastly, one of his eyes was burned away from draconic acid.

Elrohir emerged from captivity hardened. The gentle poet lies buried beneath vigilance and steel. Against most, he is cautious but fair, his old compassion flickering when he sees innocents in danger. But against dragonkind and their servants, the gentle elf is gone. In their place stands a ruthless hunter. His strikes are swift and merciless, his judgment absolute. To Elrohir, dragonkin represent everything that shattered his former life, and for them he reserves no mercy.

Even so, fragments of the old Elrohir endure. When he feels safe—rare moments shared with trusted companions—he may still hum a song, pen a quiet verse, or let his guard drop long enough to admire a star-filled sky. These glimpses reveal the gentle elf he once was, and perhaps the one he longs to become again.
### Hooks
- Primary drive: return to Silvanesti and learn the fate of his homeland and loved ones.  
- Moments of safety reveal glimpses of his former gentle self.  
- Ruthlessness against dragonkin can endanger allies if unchecked.  
- Physical scars influence pride and self-image, shaping resilience.  
- Survivor's guilt fuels loyalty to the defenseless and moral challenges.

## Davos

**Race:** Human  
**Background:** Mage of High Sorcery  
**Class:** Sorcerer (Lunar)
**Starting player:** Lisa

![[lunar-sorcerer.jpg]]
### Backstory

Davos grew up in desperate poverty, the eldest of three siblings in a family that often went without food. His parents were kind but powerless; it fell to Davos to keep his younger brother and sister alive. His early life was shaped by hunger, scarcity, and the knowledge that survival required cunning as much as strength.

From a young age, strange energies stirred within him. He could feel the pulse of the moons, their light bending to his will in ways he couldn't explain. Where others saw only shadows, Davos could weave strands of silver and crimson radiance into flickering patterns. But though his natural gift was extraordinary, Davos was impatient, lazy with study, and disinterested in slow lessons. He preferred to test the edges of his abilities on his own terms, often taking dangerous risks.

His potential eventually caught the attention of the Mages of High Sorcery, who offered him a place within the Towers. There, Davos was introduced to the three Orders—White, Red, and Black—each one claiming their philosophy was the true way to wield magic responsibly. He discovered that the moons themselves shifted his abilities, waxing and waning with their phases, and so too did his temptations: noble aspirations under Solinari, pragmatic cunning under Lunitari, ambition and hunger under Nuitari.

He has already completed his First Trial of the Barb, where his raw brilliance shone through. Instead of confronting the trial as expected, Davos exploited loopholes and bent the rules of the test, demonstrating both cunning and a willingness to twist convention to his advantage. The Masters recognized his strength but whispered of his dangerous tendencies.

Davos remains tethered to his family, sending them coin and supplies whenever possible. His siblings see him as their savior, though they do not understand the political and spiritual battles raging within him. His greatest fear is failing them—sliding into obscurity, or worse, corruption, and leaving them to starve as they once did.

Now, as his training continues, Davos is assigned as a trainee-advisor to the Knights of Solamnia, where his genius as a strategist can be tested. Yet beneath the surface lies the deeper struggle: to endure the Trials of High Sorcery and decide what kind of mage he will become.
### Hooks

Trials test skill, morality, and identity.  
- Trial I – The Barb (passed): resisted illusions, survived hostile environments, exploited loopholes. 
- Trial II – The Heart: tests loyalty and compassion versus power.  
- Trial III – The Veil: confront shadow self, accepting or destroying inner darkness.  

| Order                  | Convictions & Beliefs                             | Actions That Lead There                                                                | Temptations / Appeals                                            |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| White Robes (Solinari) | Magic is a sacred gift for justice and protection | Sacrifice wealth, status, safety to aid others; resist shortcuts; protect innocents    | Family struggles tug at compassion; praised by Paladine's allies |
| Red Robes (Lunitari)   | Knowledge and balance; pursuit of understanding   | Clever compromises, exploiting loopholes, moral gray choices                           | Whispers freedom through knowledge; rules are chains             |
| Black Robes (Nuitari)  | Magic is power; morality is weakness              | Pursue magic for security or ambition; betray allies; prioritize family over strangers | Poverty and trauma tempt selfishness; Nuitari offers greatness   |

## Worm

**Race:** Dragonborn (Black)
**Background:** Farmer
**Class:** Cleric
**Starting player:** Loic

![[aj966qp76ek61.jpg]]
### Backstory

Worm's story begins in strangeness. As a child, he was cast into Krynn during the great collision of worlds, a fate he neither understood nor controlled. From the moment of his arrival, whispers clung to him. The smallfolk, still haunted by dragon myths and the silence of the gods, called him a bad omen. Scholars and sages studied him with suspicion, unsettled by the subtle chromatic cast to his skin and eyes—traits he himself did not understand. To some, he was a harbinger of doom. To others, a messiah sent to break the godless silence.

It was that last belief which drew the attention of a faction of radical devotees of Paladine. In their fanatic search for signs, they seized upon Worm as a supposed vessel of prophecy. For a time, he was paraded as an omen of the god's return, treated as both idol and prisoner. But as their zeal grew more twisted, their faith in him soured. They branded him unworthy, a fraud, a living blasphemy. When their obsession curdled into hatred, they cast him off. Isolated, despised, and stripped of any protection, Worm was left vulnerable.

Rumors of the "god-touched boy” soon reached darker ears. He was captured and sold through the criminal underworld, paraded on a slave block where his strange features and cursed reputation marked him as both curiosity and liability. His buyers were the infamous Bakaris Uth Estide the Elder and his son, Bakaris the Younger.

To Worm, they were two sides of the same cruelty. The Elder, calculating and pitiless, saw him as little more than property—a tool to be worked until broken. The Younger was worse in many ways: arrogant, vain, and eager to prove himself through violence, he delighted in humiliating Worm, taking out his temper and frustrations on the boy whenever he could. Among the stables and muck, Worm was reduced to less than human, a slave in all but name, stripped of dignity and treated as a plaything for their malice.
### Hooks

- Worm is a vessel between mortal and divine, light and darkness.  
- Paladine: radiant visions demanding resilience, justice, and selflessness.  
- Takhisis: shadowed comfort, offering power, control, and revenge.  
- Worm's choices could affect the balance between Light and Darkness in Krynn.
